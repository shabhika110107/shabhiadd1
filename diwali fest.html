About Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most celebrated festivals in India. It's known as the "Festival of Lights," symbolizing the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Diwali is celebrated with beautiful lights, vibrant decorations, fireworks, and delicious sweets.

How Diwali is Celebrated

Activity Description
Lighting Diyas Small lamps called diyas are lit around the home to signify the victory of light.
Rangoli Colorful patterns made on the floor with colored powders, flowers, or sand.
Fireworks Fireworks displays to celebrate the festive spirit and joy of Diwali.
Festive Sweets Various sweets like ladoos, barfis, and jalebis are shared among friends and family.

Diwali Traditions

Diwali has rich cultural traditions. People clean and decorate their homes, wear new clothes, and prepare a variety of sweets. Diyas and lamps are lit everywhere, and families gather to celebrate the occasion together. It is a time of joy, sharing, and coming together with loved ones.
